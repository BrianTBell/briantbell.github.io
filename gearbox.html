Gearbox Prototype

For this project, I designed and analyzed a 75x spur reduction gearbox to lift a 20kg mass vertically, as specified by the QUT EGB210 Teaching Team. The design process involved creating a three-stage spur gearbox prototype using acrylic, aluminum, and plywood, with laser-cut components and hand-assembled parts. I conducted theoretical hand calculations and SolidWorks modeling to evaluate gear and shaft performance under static and fatigue loading, ensuring compliance with client requirements for perpetual use over two years. The prototype was tested with a torque meter and varying loads (5kg to 20kg) to assess efficiency and identify failure points.

Outcomes

The three-stage spur gearbox prototype achieved a 73.52x reduction ratio, meeting the client's 75x (Â±2.5) requirement, with a maximum tested efficiency of 72.67%. Hand calculations and AGMA fatigue analysis revealed that most gears required increased face widths (up to 45.6mm for Gear E) to ensure a safety factor above 1.5 for two-year perpetual use. Shafts, made from AISI 4140 steel, exhibited safety factors above 2.95 under critical loading. Testing identified pulley keyway shear as the primary failure mode, addressed in the final design by implementing flush steel keyways. The design validated theoretical calculations but highlighted alignment issues impacting efficiency, suggesting improvements in material quality and manufacturing precision.
