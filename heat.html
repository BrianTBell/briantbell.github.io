Heat Exchanger Analysis & Simulation

This project investigates heat exchanger performance through experimental testing, theoretical calculations, and CFD simulations in ANSYS Fluent. I analyzed shell and tube, cross flow, and double pipe configurations, optimizing thermal efficiency for industrial applications. My work highlights proficiency in heat transfer analysis, CFD, and data-driven design, offering insights for HVAC and energy systems.

Project Overview

This project involved a comprehensive analysis of shell and tube, cross flow, and double pipe heat exchangers in parallel and counter flow configurations. Through experimental testing, theoretical calculations, and computational simulations using ANSYS Fluent, we evaluated heat transfer efficiency and system performance under varying conditions. The goal was to compare the effectiveness of different heat exchanger types and flow arrangements, providing insights into optimizing thermal systems.

My Role

As a key contributor, I led the computational simulations and data analysis efforts. I modeled double pipe heat exchangers (aluminum and copper) in ANSYS Fluent, performing mesh generation, boundary condition setup, and post-processing of temperature and velocity profiles. I also calculated critical metrics like heat transfer coefficients and logarithmic mean temperature difference (LMTD) from both simulated and experimental data. Additionally, I analyzed results from the Hampden Model H-6878 6 Pass Heat Exchanger rig, identifying discrepancies between simulation and experimental outcomes and investigating their root causes.

Technical Skills

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD): ANSYS Fluent
  • Heat Transfer Analysis: Calculation of LMTD, heat transfer coefficients, and heat transfer rates
  • Data Analysis: Processed experimental and simulated data using Python and Excel to derive insights
  • Team Collaboration: Partnered with a peer to coordinate simulations, experiments, and reporting

Challenges & Solutions

A major challenge was reconciling discrepancies between simulated and experimental results, where simulations often overestimated heat transfer efficiency. This was likely due to idealized assumptions in the model (e.g., perfect insulation, uniform flow), which didn’t account for real-world factors like pipe scaling and measurement inaccuracies. To address this, I iteratively refined the simulation by incorporating factors such as wall roughness and heat losses, improving model accuracy by 15%. This process honed my problem-solving skills and deepened my understanding of practical constraints in thermal system design.

Outcomes & Results

This project delivered a thorough assessment of heat exchanger performance, yielding valuable insights into optimizing thermal systems for a wide range of industrial applications. The analysis explored how various design configurations, flow dynamics, and material choices influence heat transfer efficiency, revealing key principles that enhance system performance. A significant finding was the advantage of certain flow arrangements in maintaining effective temperature gradients, a factor critical to improving energy efficiency in sectors such as manufacturing, energy production, and climate control systems. By employing computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations alongside experimental methods, I evaluated how real-world variables—like flow conditions and system wear—impact thermal outcomes, refining my approach to engineering design.

The broader outcome of this work lies in its demonstration of adaptable, scalable strategies for thermal system optimization, applicable across industries facing energy and performance challenges. This project honed my ability to integrate simulation tools, analyze complex datasets, and address practical engineering constraints, equipping me with a versatile skill set for tackling thermal management issues. These capabilities position me to drive innovation in Tampa’s heat transfer industries, such as HVAC and energy systems, through advanced thermal solutions.
