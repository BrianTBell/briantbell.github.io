Overview

Capability summary

I own post-award engineering execution for regulated electromechanical hardware programs, including wire harnesses and box-build assemblies, within an AS9100-controlled environment. My role begins once a project is awarded. From that point, I translate the contract, production requirements, and First Article obligations into an executable engineering plan.

This work connects engineering, procurement, suppliers, test labs, quality, manufacturing, shipping, and customer-facing submission requirements into a single controlled execution flow.

Challenge

The execution challenge

Post-award regulated hardware execution requires careful translation of contract requirements, technical specifications, production deliverables, and First Article obligations into a plan that can actually be built, sourced, tested, inspected, documented, and released.

The challenge is not isolated design work. It is coordinating long-lead procurement, component availability, supplier quoting, BOM readiness, fixture needs, inspection planning, internal testing, third-party labs, quality review, customer submission, and production handoff without losing schedule control.

Scope

What I own

Engineering Execution

Develop and maintain the production BOM, create or update part records, initiate requisitions, resolve obsolete or unavailable components, coordinate supplier quoting, and support sourcing decisions for commercial and custom-fabricated parts.

Support Hardware

When test fixtures or support hardware are required, define the need, develop the design, release the documentation, and drive procurement so testing and production readiness are not blocked.

First Article

First Article Test & Release

A major part of this responsibility is First Article Test & Release. I break down contractual test requirements into clear procedures, draft the First Article Test Report, prepare supporting inspection documentation, and coordinate the internal and external testing required to complete the submission.

This includes determining which tests can be performed in-house, which require third-party labs, and how procurement, inspection, testing, and reporting must be sequenced to avoid delays.

Execution

Approach

01 · Interpret

Contract and production requirements

Translate customer requirements, technical specifications, deliverable expectations, and First Article obligations into an actionable engineering plan.

02 · Structure

BOM, records, requisitions, and sourcing

Maintain the production BOM, create and update part records, initiate requisitions, resolve sourcing issues, and coordinate supplier quoting for commercial and custom-fabricated parts.

03 · Prepare

Fixtures, support hardware, and test readiness

Define fixture needs, release support hardware documentation, drive procurement, and align inspection and test preparation with First Article requirements.

04 · Execute

Testing, reporting, and submission package

Draft procedures, prepare the First Article Test Report, coordinate internal and external testing, and package the documentation required for customer and quality review.

05 · Handoff

Production readiness and release

Revise the production BOM, identify long-lead risks, prepare production requisitions, and coordinate with Quality, Manufacturing Engineering, and Shipping to support customer submission and production handoff.

Review

Customer and DCMA support

As First Article completion approaches, I support customer-facing submission activities and help transition the program toward production readiness. I also support in-person DCMA review of completed First Article hardware and final reports alongside Quality.

This requires the engineering package, hardware condition, inspection evidence, test results, and report structure to align before formal review, reducing avoidable churn at the point of customer or government inspection.

Result

Outcome

The result is a controlled path from contract award through First Article approval and production release. My work connects engineering, procurement, suppliers, test labs, quality, manufacturing, and shipping into a single execution flow for regulated hardware programs.
