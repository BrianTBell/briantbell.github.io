Conceptual Development & Design of Subterranean Rover

I led a team in designing an unmanned rover for a simulated mine rescue mission, focusing on optimizing its mobility and functionality. Using SolidWorks for 3D modeling, ANSYS for finite element analysis (FEA), and detailed hand calculations, we developed a robust design capable of navigating challenging terrain. My responsibilities included overseeing concept development and conducting stress analysis. The result was a successful rover equipped with a bio-pack solution, demonstrating my skills in mechanical design, simulation, and project management. This project showcases my ability to lead technical teams and deliver innovative solutions under complex constraints.

Project Overview

This aim of this project was to present a hypothetical scenario to a group of undergraduate engineers and challenge them to develop their own solution. The scenario involved a mine collapse with limited access, requiring the delivery of a bio-pack to ensure the minersâ€™ survival until a more comprehensive extraction plan could be implemented. Our student team was provided with the specifications of a rover chassis, which we could modify as needed to make it capable of delivering the bio-pack. However, beyond this there was little help provided in terms of design development. Throughout the process, several interim deliverables were requested by our teaching team. Challenges & Solutions

1. Apathetic Teammates

Challenge: Teammates we're not enthusiastic and did not care about project outcomes.

Solution: I understood the root cause can't be changed, but reinforced that the project is an opportunity to make something really cool. Beyond that I maintained clear expectations and deadlines, provided support when available, and tried to engange the team in decision making and let them own their contributions.

2. Navigation & Stability Enhancements

Challenge: The rover needed to navigate a complex underground environment with uneven terrain and the potential for overturning.

Solution: A self-righting strategy was devised using controlled movement and weight distribution. Noted that future designs should integrate active stabilization systems to enhance terrain adaptability.

3. Sensor & Vision Optimization

Challenge: Test simulations showed the onboard camera system required improved visibility and angle detection to enhance situational awareness in low-light conditions.

Solution: Noted that a refined sensor suite, including an inclinometer and optimized lighting, should be introduced to improve terrain assessment and miner detection.

4. Structural Resilience & Impact Protection

Challenge: The rover had to withstand drops and navigation of rough terrain without compromising critical components such as the lift system.

Solution: Structural elements were iteratively redesigned to enhance durability. Noted that future iterations should undergo more extensive impact testing to optimize material selection.

5. Real-World Operational Considerations

Challenge: The rover's power management, communication reliability, and adaptability to unforeseen obstacles were key considerations for real-world deployment.

Solution: These considerations were put aside for the sake of the class project. However their relevance, and the implications of their omission was reflected on in detail.

Outcomes & Results

A modified rover was designed as a working solution to the hypothetical mine collapse. The final rover design was tested via simulation, and sucessefully delivered the biopack to the miners as needed. In a broader sense I got to oversee the design of a Mechanical Engineering System from start to end (kind of) and I'm proud of the contributions I made.
