What I Do

Contract-to-Production Execution

I support programs from contract award through First Article completion and transition into production, with emphasis on turning requirements into controlled, testable, releasable hardware.

  • Transform contract and customer requirements into engineering deliverables, build plans, and verification obligations
  • Create and maintain production MBOM structures; support procurement sequencing and long-lead risk mitigation
  • Evaluate part availability/obsolescence; support alternates and risk-buys to protect schedule and build readiness
  • Drive configuration-controlled releases through ECNs and controlled documentation packages
  • Coordinate Quality FAI package completion and support production transition readiness
  • Create new parts, maintain manufacturing BOMs, update item masters, and process material requisitions/purchase requests in SAP


First Article Test & Release (FATR) and Verification

I own First Article Test & Release execution at the program level: planning, procedures, coordination, documentation, and release readiness.

  • Author FATR plans and reports aligned to released drawings and contractual requirements
  • Develop verification procedures; decide in-house vs third-party lab execution approach based on schedule, capability, and requirements
  • Design/source test fixtures where needed; coordinate test setup, execution, documentation, and results packaging
  • Manage variance workflows (RFVs) and support customer technical alignment as required

Electrical verification and acceptance testing (procedures + execution support)

  • Continuity, DC resistance, Insulation resistance, Chassis isolation, Dielectric withstand (hi-pot), Circuit-breaker functional verification

Environmental testing (plans/reports + execution coordination)

  • Shock, Vibration, Thermal (low / high / extreme high), Humidity, Salt Fog, Chemical Exposure, Corrosion, Fungus, Submergence, Steam / water jet


Mechanical Design and Build Feedback

I support mechanical release and R&D redesign efforts using build/test feedback, prototype iteration, and configuration-controlled documentation.

  • Oversee R&D design/prototyping projects including system design and research, technical documentation, task delegation, and technical troubleshooting (CAD, drafting, coding, soldering, part sourcing, reporting/status briefings)
  • Led mechanical redesign of a multi-level assembly with hundreds of parts, including alignment to existing part catalogs and issuance of new part numbers consistent with existing numbering schemes
  • Create new CAD models, quote and procure new parts, and assemble prototype builds for evaluation
  • Derive required test procedures and reports from product descriptions/requirements to document engineering due diligence and support warranty basis
  • Author test procedures through ECN, record prototype test data directly into controlled procedures, finalize test reports, and issue final ECNs
  • Issue and revise production drawings in SolidWorks based on test results and manufacturing feedback
  • Perform hands-on assembly of select hardware to validate tolerances and assembly sequences prior to release
  • Reflow profiling and high-reliability solder process support aligned to Class 3 requirements (IPC-A-610 / J-STD-001)
