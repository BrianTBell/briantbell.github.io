Robotics R&D

Cobot-Enabled Workcell Platform

I served as Technical Lead for a regulated R&D program developing a commercial, high-precision dual-cobot workcell platform. The system was designed to automate tedious, precision technician workflows while improving repeatability, throughput, and process control. My role spanned the full system, including mechanical design, electromechanical integration, controls, software architecture, testing, procurement, and technical execution.


A major portion of my work involved developing the Python-based control software that coordinated robot behavior, subsystem sequencing, operator interaction, and process flow. I helped define the software architecture and worked hands-on with robot communication, state-machine logic, GUI concepts, sensor integration, and functional test routines. In parallel, I owned workcell layout, Universal Robots integration, custom CAD adaptations, electrical setup, subsystem power-rating review, and off-the-shelf component integration.


Beyond the technical implementation, I drove execution deadlines, supported procurement and requisitioning, led project demonstrations, and coordinated verification activities across accuracy, precision, cycle time, and functional performance. I regularly briefed C-level leadership, supported client technical interchange, and contributed to proposals, feasibility studies, internal technical reports, and monthly customer status reporting. The effort resulted in a feasible commercialized prototype platform that reduced manual effort, increased throughput, and demonstrated the viability of the automation approach.
