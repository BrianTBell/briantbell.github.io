Overview

Capability summary

I led the design, redesign, and prototyping of regulated defense electromechanical hardware, serving as the lead engineer and primary mechanical owner across the effort. My scope covered enclosures, assemblies, mechanisms, fixtures, interfaces, subsystem integration, and controlled release requirements.

I owned the work from concept development through prototype build, troubleshooting, verification support, and production handoff. Specific program details are intentionally generalized due to the regulated nature of the work.

Challenge

The engineering challenge

Regulated hardware programs require more than successful design intent. The work must move from concept to physical build while maintaining fit, function, manufacturability, documentation control, supplier readiness, verification evidence, and production handoff discipline.

The practical challenge was executing quickly across mechanical design, sourcing, prototype build, troubleshooting, test support, and release documentation while maintaining the rigor expected in a controlled engineering environment.

Scope

What I owned

Design & Release

Created and maintained SolidWorks models, drawings, part records, MBOMs, ECNs, and release documentation within a controlled engineering environment.

Sourcing & Build

Drove part creation, supplier sourcing, RFQs, technical supplier coordination, procurement follow-up, manufacturability resolution, prototype assembly, and fit/function troubleshooting.

Mechanical Ownership SolidWorks Drawings MBOMs ECNs Supplier Coordination Prototype Build Verification Support Production Handoff
Execution

Approach

01 · Define

Concept, interfaces, and subsystem scope

Defined and refined mechanical scope across enclosures, assemblies, mechanisms, fixtures, interfaces, subsystem integration needs, and controlled release requirements.

02 · Design

CAD, drawings, records, and release structure

Created and maintained SolidWorks models, drawings, part records, MBOMs, ECNs, and supporting release documentation needed for controlled engineering execution.

03 · Build

Hands-on prototype execution

Personally built prototypes, validated assembly procedures, resolved fit and function issues, and acted as the technician when required to keep the program moving.

04 · Verify

Test planning and documentation

Planned and documented verification activities including functional testing, environmental testing, reliability evaluation, and fit checks.

05 · Handoff

Manufacturing readiness and production transition

Closed open design and manufacturing issues, supported procurement readiness, finalized controlled documentation, and prepared hardware packages for production handoff.

Verification

Test and release support

I planned and documented verification activities for regulated hardware, including functional testing, environmental testing, reliability evaluation, and fit checks. This work supported controlled engineering decisions, manufacturing readiness, and release confidence.

The emphasis was practical and evidence-driven: validate that the hardware could be built, assembled, tested, documented, and transitioned into production with open issues resolved.

Result

Outcome

The work resulted in released designs, validated prototypes, completed test documentation, resolved manufacturing issues, and production-ready hardware packages for regulated programs.

Confidentiality note: specific program names, product details, customer details, and controlled technical information are intentionally generalized because this work supported regulated defense programs.
